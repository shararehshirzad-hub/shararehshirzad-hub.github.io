Sustainable & Smart Infrastructure Materials

Biochar-engineered concrete, self-sensing and self-healing materials, and advanced asphalt binders.

I am an Assistant Professor and Program Director (Building Science) in the Department of Sustainable Technology and the Built Environment at Appalachian State University.

My research focuses on sustainable and smart infrastructure materials, including self-healing asphalt and biochar-engineered concrete for low-carbon, resilient structural systems, combining experimental testing, data-driven modeling, and industry collaboration.

Research Areas

Biochar Concrete

Low-carbon cementitious systems using waste-derived biochar and SCMs to improve performance and sustainability.

Self-Sensing Concrete

Piezoresistive cementitious composites using conductive/hybrid fillers for distributed monitoring.

Sustainable Construction Materials

Circular material strategies and performance-based evaluation for resilient infrastructure.

Polyurethane-Modified Asphalt

Binder modification to improve rheology, mechanical performance, and durability.

Self-Healing Asphalt Materials

Light-activated and healing-agent approaches to improve crack resistance and extend pavement service life.

Data & Modeling

Predictive modeling to connect materials design, microstructure, and performance outcomes.
